Here’s where you’ll find details about your electricity usage for this period

  • • Meter – your home’s unique meter number.
  • • Service Period – the dates covered by this month’s usage.
  • • Current Meter Read – the number shown on this period’s reading.
  • • Prior Meter Read – the number shown on your meter during the previous period’s reading.
  • • Multiplier – some meters only measure a fraction or multiple of the actual electricity used, so a meter multiplier (also known as a meter constant or Kh) converts the reading into the correct amount of electricity consumed in kilowatt-hours (kWh).

For example, if a meter has a multiplier of 5, it means that each unit on the meter (i.e., one revolution of the dial) represents 5 kWh of electricity consumption. So if the meter reading is 100 units, the actual electricity used would be 100 units multiplied by the meter multiplier of 5, totaling 500 kWh.

  • Most residential properties won’t require a multiplier, so it would appear as “1” on your bill.
  • • kWh Usage – this is the amount of electricity you used (shown in kWh) times the price you pay per kWh.
  • • Estimated Read — if we weren’t able to take a meter read during this billing cycle, your reading may be estimated based on your average usage.
